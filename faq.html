Everything you need to know about ClaimReceipts

ClaimReceipts is a public tracker for bold predictions made by tech leaders, crypto influencers, and public figures. We document claims with deadlines and track whether they come true or not.

Think of it as "receipts" for predictions ‚Äî proof of what was said, so we can hold people accountable when their forecasts don't pan out.

Bold predictions influence real decisions. When a CEO says "AI will replace all programmers in 6 months," people panic about their careers. When an influencer says "Bitcoin to $1 million," people invest their savings.

But when these predictions fail, there's usually no accountability. The same people make new predictions and the cycle continues. We're here to track the record.

üç∑ Aged Like Wine ‚Äî Claims that were proven TRUE. Like wine, they got better with time.

  • Who ‚Äî Person or organization name
  • What ‚Äî The exact claim or quote
  • When ‚Äî Date the claim was made
  • Deadline ‚Äî When it should happen by
  • Source ‚Äî Link to the original statement (video, article, tweet)

We review all submissions for accuracy before adding them.

We accept claims that are:

  • Specific ‚Äî Clear enough to verify (not vague like "AI will change everything")
  • Time-bound ‚Äî Has a deadline or timeframe
  • Verifiable ‚Äî Can be objectively checked
  • Sourced ‚Äî Made publicly with documented evidence

We currently focus on AI/LLM, Bitcoin/Crypto, and Tech predictions.

We primarily track claims from public figures ‚Äî CEOs, founders, analysts, and influencers with significant reach. This is because their predictions have outsized influence on public opinion and markets.

That said, if you're a notable figure in your field and want to put your prediction on record, feel free to submit it!

We wait for the deadline to pass, then evaluate based on objective evidence:

  • TRUE ‚Äî The prediction happened as described
  • FALSE ‚Äî The prediction clearly did not happen
  • PENDING ‚Äî Deadline hasn't passed yet
  • EXPIRED ‚Äî Deadline passed, unclear outcome (we may update later)

We try to be generous with interpretation ‚Äî if someone said "by end of 2025" and it happened in January 2026, we might still count it. But "Bitcoin to $1 million by 2020" when it hit $29K? That's a clear FALSE.

We're human and can make mistakes! If you believe a verdict is incorrect, email us at corrections@claimreceipts.com with:

  • Which claim you're disputing
  • Why you think the verdict is wrong
  • Evidence supporting your position

We take accuracy seriously and will review all disputes.

Trust Score = (Total Claims - False Claims) / Total Claims √ó 100%

For example, if someone made 5 predictions and 2 were proven false, their trust score is (5-2)/5 = 60%.

Note: Trust scores are only as good as our data. With more tracked claims, scores become more meaningful. We're still early, so take these with a grain of salt!
