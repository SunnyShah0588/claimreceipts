Why accountability matters in the age of hype

ü§î Why We Built This

Every day, tech CEOs, crypto influencers, and industry leaders make bold predictions. "AI will replace all programmers in 6 months!" "Bitcoin to $1 million by next year!" "Full self-driving by 2020!"

These claims generate headlines, move markets, and influence decisions. But what happens when the deadline passes? Usually... nothing. The world moves on, the prediction is forgotten, and the same people make new bold claims.

üßæ We're keeping the receipts.

ClaimReceipts is a public archive of predictions with deadlines. We track what was said, when it was said, and whether it came true. Because accountability shouldn't be optional.

üåç Our Mission

We believe that collective trust matters. When leaders can say anything without consequence, it erodes our ability to make informed decisions. Hype cycles distort reality. FOMO drives poor choices.

By tracking predictions over time, we hope to:

  • Encourage critical thinking about bold claims
  • Build a historical record of predictions in tech & crypto
  • Help people identify who's worth listening to
  • Add a small dose of accountability to public discourse

üìä What We Track

We focus on claims that are:

  • Specific - Clear enough to be proven true or false
  • Time-bound - Has a deadline or timeframe
  • Verifiable - Can be checked against reality
  • Sourced - Made publicly with documented evidence

Our current focus areas are AI/LLM predictions, Bitcoin & crypto forecasts, and tech industry claims. We may expand to other areas in the future.
